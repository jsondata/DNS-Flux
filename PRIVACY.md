# Privacy Policy for DNS-Flux

Last Updated: January 29, 2026

**DNS-Flux** is committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how our Chrome extension handles information.

## 1. Information Collection and Use
**DNS-Flux DOES NOT collect, store, or transmit any personal data or browsing information.**
- We do not have access to your browsing history.
- We do not use any tracking or analytics tools.
- We do not transmit any data to external servers.

## 2. Permissions Justification
All permissions requested by DNS-Flux are used solely for the technical operation of the extension:
- **tabs**: To identify the active tab and trigger a page reload after flushing DNS.
- **storage**: To save your local preferences (e.g., auto-refresh interval).
- **alarms**: To schedule periodic DNS flush tasks per your settings.
- **contextMenus**: To provide a quick entry in the right-click menu.
- **offscreen**: To communicate with the internal `chrome.benchmarking` API.

## 3. Data Storage
All settings are stored locally on your device via the `chrome.storage.local` API and are never uploaded to the cloud.

## 4. Contact
If you have any questions, please visit our project page: https://github.com/jsondata/DNS-Flux

This extension adheres to the Chrome Web Store User Data Policy.
